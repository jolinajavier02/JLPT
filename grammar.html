Comprehensive guide from Survival to Professional Japanese.

1. Sentence Basics

Japanese follows a Subject + Object + Verb (SOV) structure. Particles mark the role of each word.

Structure: [Subject] は [Object] を [Verb]
Particles: は (Topic), を (Object), に (Direction/Time), で (Location), へ (Direction).

Examples

1. 私は寿司を食べます。
(I eat sushi.)

2. 田中さんは本を読みます。
(Mr. Tanaka reads a book.)

3. 私は水を飲みます。
(I drink water.)

4. 先生は日本語を話します。
(The teacher speaks Japanese.)

5. 私は映画を見ます。
(I watch a movie.)

Common Mistake

Putting the verb before the object. Incorrect: "Watashi wa tabemasu sushi."

2. Verb Basics

Verbs are divided into three groups: Ru-verbs, U-verbs, and Irregular.

Polite Form (~masu) Conjugation:
Group Dictionary Form ~masu Form
Ru-verbs Taberu (Eat) Tabe + masu = Tabemasu
U-verbs Iku (Go) Ik(i) + masu = Ikimasu
Irregular Suru (Do) Shimasu
Irregular Kuru (Come) Kimasu

Examples

1. 食べます (Eat) / 食べました (Ate)

2. 行きます (Go) / 行きません (Don't go)

3. します (Do) / しました (Did)

4. 来ます (Come) / 来ました (Came)

5. 飲みます (Drink) / 飲みました (Drank)

Real-life Usage

Essential for polite interactions with strangers and colleagues.

3. Adjectives (I vs Na)

I-adjectives end in 'i' and conjugate themselves. Na-adjectives need 'na' to connect to nouns.

Conjugation (Negative):
I-adj: Oishii → Oishi + kunai = Oishikunai
Na-adj: Kirei → Kirei + ja nai = Kirei ja nai

Examples

1. 美味しい寿司 (Delicious sushi)

2. 綺麗な花 (Beautiful flower)

3. 寒い日 (Cold day)

4. 静かな部屋 (Quiet room)

5. 高い時計 (Expensive watch)

Common Mistake

Adding 'na' to I-adjectives. Incorrect: "Oishii na sushi."

4. Question & Existence

Use Arimasu for objects and Imasu for living things. Add ka for questions.

Location: [Place] に [Subject] が あります/います。

Examples

1. 机の上に本があります。
(There is a book on the desk.)

2. 庭に猫がいます。
(There is a cat in the garden.)

3. 先生はどこにいますか？
(Where is the teacher?)

4. 駅はどこですか？
(Where is the station?)

5. 車があります。
(There is a car.)

Real-life Usage

Finding items in a store or asking if someone is at home.

5. Te-form (Requests & Connection)

The Te-form is used for requests, connecting actions, and more.

Conjugation:
u/tsu/ru → tte | mu/bu/nu → nde | ku → ite | gu → ide | su → shite

Examples

1. ちょっと待ってください。
(Please wait a moment.)

2. 食べてください。
(Please eat.)

3. 行ってください。
(Please go.)

4. してください。
(Please do.)

5. 飲んでください。
(Please drink.)

Permission

~te mo ii desu ka? (May I...?)
