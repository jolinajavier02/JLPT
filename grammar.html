Grammar Mastery

Comprehensive guide from Survival to Professional Japanese.

1. Sentence Basics

Japanese follows a Subject + Object + Verb (SOV) structure. Particles mark the role of each word.

Structure: [Subject] は [Object] を [Verb]

Example

私は寿司を食べます。

Watashi wa sushi o tabemasu. (I eat sushi.)

Common Mistake

Putting the verb before the object. Incorrect: "Watashi wa tabemasu sushi."

2. Verb Basics

Verbs are divided into three groups: Ru-verbs, U-verbs, and Irregular (Suru/Kuru).

Polite Form (~masu): Used for daily conversation and work.

Present / Past

Tabemasu (Eat) / Tabemashita (Ate)

Ikimasu (Go) / Ikimasen (Don't go)

Real-life Usage

Essential for polite interactions with strangers and colleagues.

3. Adjectives (I vs Na)

I-adjectives end in 'i' (Oishii). Na-adjectives need 'na' to connect to nouns (Kirei na).

Comparison: [A] より [B] のほうが [Adj] です。
(B is more [Adj] than A.)

Example

猫より犬のほうが好きです。

Neko yori inu no hou ga suki desu. (I like dogs more than cats.)

Common Mistake

Adding 'na' to I-adjectives. Incorrect: "Oishii na sushi."

4. Question & Existence

Use Arimasu for objects and Imasu for living things. Add ka for questions.

Location: [Place] に [Subject] が あります/います。

Example

机の上に本があります。

Tsukue no ue ni hon ga arimasu. (There is a book on the desk.)

Real-life Usage

Finding items in a store or asking if someone is at home.

5. Te-form (Requests & Connection)

The Te-form is the most versatile form. It's used for requests, connecting actions, and more.

Request: Verb [Te-form] + ください (kudasai)

Example

ちょっと待ってください。

Chotto matte kudasai. (Please wait a moment.)

Permission

~te mo ii desu ka? (May I...?)

6. Desire & Intention

Express what you want to do with ~tai and what you plan to do with ~tsumori.

Example

日本に行きたいです。

Nihon ni ikitai desu. (I want to go to Japan.)

Advice

~hou ga ii desu (You should...)

7. Casual vs Polite Speech

Japanese uses different levels of politeness. Use Polite with strangers and Casual with friends.

Polite

Ikimasu (I go)

Oishii desu (It's delicious)

Casual

Iku (I go)

Oishii (It's delicious)

8. Keigo (Honorifics)

Used in professional settings to show respect. Includes Sonkeigo (Exalting) and Kenjougo (Humble).

Standard Phrase: お疲れ様です (Otsukaresama desu) - Thank you for your hard work.

Business Usage

Essential for work, customer service, and formal emails.

Common Mistake

Using Sonkeigo for your own actions. (Very rude!)
